# Case Study: Marketing Foundation for a Cloud Cost Governance Platform

---

## The Situation

A Series A cloud cost governance platform was entering a crowded market with two established competitors, Vantage and CloudZero, holding significant share. The company had no dedicated marketing team and no content infrastructure in place. Without a clear SEO strategy or homepage messaging, they had limited ability to attract and convert the buyers they were built for.

---

## The Brief

The client needed a complete marketing foundation, delivered fast, that would let a small team compete on organic search, convert two distinct buyer types, and produce content consistently without requiring constant strategic input.

---

## What Was Delivered

| Deliverable | Summary |
|---|---|
| SEO and Keyword Strategy | 18 prioritized keywords across 4 intent tiers, competitive gap analysis against both category leaders, a 90-day content roadmap, and an internal linking framework |
| Homepage Messaging | 3 hero copy alternatives, a dual-audience framework for CTO and Finance buyers, recommended section architecture, and 4 A/B test hypotheses with measurable outcomes |
| 30-Day Content Calendar | 30 fully specified entries covering blog, LinkedIn, email, and video formats, mapped to keywords, buyer intent stages, and distribution channels |
| LinkedIn Thought Leadership | 6 distinct angles for CTO and Finance audiences, including two that challenge FinOps convention and one built specifically for the underserved CFO buyer |

---

## Selected Results

- Identified "cloud cost governance" as a high-value category keyword neither Vantage nor CloudZero held authority on, giving the client a realistic path to organic visibility without competing for terms the leaders already dominate.
- Delivered a 30-day content calendar sized for a two-person team, with every entry fully specified so no additional planning work was required before execution.
- Produced three homepage hero variants and a dual-audience messaging framework that gave the sales team a competitive positioning statement they could use immediately, not just a marketing document.
- The full engagement, from brief to four delivered workstreams, was completed in under 48 hours with a QA score of 95 out of 100.
