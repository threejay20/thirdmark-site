FAQ

Common Questions

The price seems high. What am I actually paying for?

You're paying for the output, not the inputs. A traditional agency at this price point charges for a team of generalists, account coordination overhead, and process theater. What you get here is a senior-level deliverable, scoped precisely, with one accountable point for quality from start to finish. If the deliverable doesn't meet the standard, it doesn't ship.

How do I know the quality will hold up across functions I've never tested?

The proof section above covers four distinct deliverable types across a single 48-hour window: keyword research, homepage messaging, a full content calendar, and LinkedIn direction. The standard is consistent because the quality review process is consistent, regardless of the function.

What's the actual turnaround on a Sprint?

3 to 7 business days for most Sprint engagements. More complex deliverables may run the full 7 days. Timeline is confirmed during scoping, before any work begins.

Who owns the work when it's delivered?

You do. Full ownership transfers on delivery for all Sprint engagements. Retainer and Studio clients own all work product produced during the engagement. No licensing arrangements, no ongoing dependencies.

I've seen AI-assisted work before and it wasn't good. Why would this be different?

Most AI-assisted work fails at the judgment layer: knowing what to make, for whom, at what level of precision, and whether it's actually right. The delivery system here handles volume and speed. The judgment layer handles scope, strategy, quality review, and the standards that determine whether something ships. Every CloudHarbor deliverable went through a structured review before it shipped. That standard applies to every engagement.
